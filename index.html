Visualized Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) levels across San Francisco to identify optimal zones for solar panel installations. High-GHI areas — rendered in red and orange — mark locations capable of producing electricity most efficiently year-round, supporting urban energy planning decisions.

Environmental Analysis · Southern Oregon

Vegetation Carbon Stock Distribution

Raster analysis mapping the spatial distribution of above-ground vegetation carbon stock (t/ha) across Lane & Douglass Counties in Oregon. A red-to-green gradient reveals high-carbon forest zones, supporting carbon sequestration assessment and climate planning.

ArcGIS Pro Raster Analysis Environmental GIS Climate

Remote Sensing Analysis · Rhode Island

NDWI — Normalized Difference Water Index

Raster analysis mapping surface water presence and vegetation moisture across Rhode Island using the Normalized Difference Water Index. A five-class gradient from yellow (dry land) to blue (open water) reveals the state's complex coastal inlets, inland ponds, and wetland systems — supporting watershed and land cover analysis.

ArcGIS Pro Remote Sensing Raster Analysis Water Resources

Remote Sensing Analysis · Ethiopia

TVDI — East & West Harerghe Drought Analysis

Temperature Vegetation Dryness Index (TVDI) analysis for East and West Harerghe regions in Ethiopia (March 2015). Derived from remote sensing data, this five-class map — Very Wet to Very Dry — supports drought risk assessment and regional land management planning.

Background

About Alex

I'm a GIS Analyst based in San Francisco. I've spent seven years working on projects where the data was hard to get, sometimes restricted, sometimes nonexistent — and where the output had to be good enough to brief decision-makers who couldn't afford to be wrong.

Some of that work I can show you: school construction dashboards across Nepal, thematic atlases for U.S. civil servant training, wildlife movement maps in Marin County. Some of it I can't — sensitive government work where I georeferenced pipelines by hand because the datasets didn't exist, and where security review was part of the workflow. I've learned to work with imperfect data and produce outputs people trust.

I love this field — that part is 100% true. Now I'm focused on bringing that rigor to urban systems, transportation infrastructure, and civic decision-making, where maps shape how cities move and grow.
